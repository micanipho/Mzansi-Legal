1. Project title
MzansiLegal: Multilingual AI-Powered Legal & Financial Rights Assistant for South African
Citizens
2. Problem statement
South Africa has one of the most progressive constitutions in the world, yet the vast majority of
citizens have limited understanding of both their legal rights and their financial rights. These two
domains are deeply intertwined — a retrenched worker simultaneously faces a labour law question,
a debt management crisis, and a tax question about their severance package. A tenant being
charged illegal interest by a loan shark faces both a National Credit Act violation and a housing
rights issue.
Access to professional guidance in either domain is expensive. Attorney consultation fees start at
R1,500–R3,000 per hour. Registered financial advisors charge R500–R2,000 per session. Legal Aid
South Africa is overburdened and only serves those meeting strict financial eligibility criteria. The
result is that ordinary citizens accept unlawful treatment or make poor financial decisions simply
because they do not know they are protected by law.
Compounding this is the language barrier. South Africa has 12 official languages, yet virtually all
legislation and financial education material is published only in English. A Sesotho-speaking
domestic worker in the Free State or an isiZulu-speaking tenant in KwaZulu-Natal cannot
meaningfully access the laws that protect them.
Beyond language, significant portions of the population face additional accessibility challenges.
South Africa has over 1.1 million people with visual impairments, including approximately 600,000
who are completely blind. An estimated 10–15% of South Africans have dyslexia, making dense
legal text particularly difficult to process. For all of these groups, a text-only platform is not enough.
Furthermore, millions of South Africans sign contracts every year — lease agreements, employment
contracts, credit agreements, cellphone contracts — without fully understanding what they are
agreeing to. Unfair clauses, illegal interest rates, and waived rights go undetected because ordinary
citizens lack the knowledge to spot them.
This project aims to bridge the knowledge gap, the language gap, and the accessibility gap by
building a single AI-powered platform where citizens — including those who are blind, dyslexic, or
have limited literacy — can ask questions about their legal and financial rights, upload contracts for
proactive analysis, and receive accurate, cited answers by typing or speaking in their home
language.
3. Goal
Build a unified platform that:
• Allows users to ask legal and financial questions in their home language (English, isiZulu,
Sesotho, or Afrikaans) via text or voice input

6. Multilingual architecture
The multilingual system uses a "translate-in, process, translate-out" approach. All legislation is
stored and searched in English. The AI handles language translation as part of the pipeline.
6.1 Query flow
1 User types or speaks a question in any supported language
2 If voice: audio is sent to OpenAI Whisper API for transcription
3 System detects the input language via lightweight LLM classification
4 If not English, the question is translated to English for embedding and search
5 RAG pipeline runs in English: embed → retrieve top-K chunks → build prompt
6 LLM prompt includes: "Respond in {language}. Keep Act names and section numbers in
English."
7 User receives answer in their language with English citations
8 User can tap speaker button to hear the answer via OpenAI TTS API
6.2 Why this approach works
• Embeddings remain in English — no re-embedding per language
• Search quality stays high (English embeddings are best-supported)
• Citations stay accurate (official legal text is English)
• GPT-4o handles isiZulu, Sesotho, and Afrikaans well
• Adding a new language requires zero re-indexing

9. Key features
9.1 Multilingual AI Q&A (RAG pipeline)
• User submits a legal or financial question via text or voice
• Voice input transcribed via Whisper with automatic language detection
• System translates to English if needed, generates embedding, performs semantic search
• Top relevant chunks from both legal and financial documents are retrieved
• LLM generates plain-language answer in user’s language with English citations
• Cross-domain questions answered holistically
9.2 AI contract analysis
Users upload any contract and receive proactive AI-powered analysis without asking a single
question:
• Contract type auto-detection: identifies lease, employment, credit, service contract, etc.

• Health score: overall score out of 100 based on fairness, legal compliance, transparency.
Displayed as animated gauge with traffic light (green/amber/red).
• Plain-language summary: one-paragraph summary of what the contract means, in user’s

• Red flag alerts: proactive warnings citing specific legislation. E.g. interest rate exceeding NCA

Rental Housing Act.
• Ask about this contract: follow-up chat using both contract text and legislation as RAG context.
• Supported types (MVP): employment contracts, lease/rental agreements, credit/loan
agreements, service contracts.
9.3 Voice support & accessibility
• Voice input: microphone button, Whisper API transcription, all 4 languages
• Voice output: speaker button on answers, OpenAI TTS playback. Auto-play for blind users
(configurable).
• Screen reader compatibility: ARIA labels, roles, full keyboard navigation
• Dyslexia-friendly mode: toggle for OpenDyslexic font, increased spacing and font size
• High contrast mode: respects OS-level accessibility settings
• Voice and text can be mixed freely
9.4 My Rights explorer & FAQ system
• Interactive browse experience — not a static FAQ list
• FAQs are admin-curated conversations: admin creates a question, system generates an
answer, admin reviews and publishes it as a public FAQ
• FAQs are tagged to a LegalCategory and appear on the home page, My Rights explorer, and as
related question suggestions
• Rights organised by category with expandable cards showing full explanations and legislation
citations
• Gamified knowledge score tracking how many topics the user has explored
• Each card has “Ask a follow-up”, “Listen in isiZulu”, and “Share” actions
• Same Conversation entity used for both private Q&A; and public FAQs — distinguished by
IsPublicFaq flag
9.5 Unified knowledge base
• Single vector store containing both legal and financial document chunks
• Each chunk tagged with domain, Act name, section number
• Admin uploads new documents via the analytics dashboard
• Adding a new language requires zero re-indexing

12.5 FAQ system
FAQs use the same Conversation entity as regular Q&A. The admin creates a conversation with
IsPublicFaq = true and assigns a FaqCategory. The system generates the answer, the admin
reviews and marks IsAccurate = true, and the conversation becomes publicly visible on the home
page and My Rights explorer. Regular user conversations have IsPublicFaq = false and are private
to the user.

13. Disclaimers & ethics
The platform provides legal and financial information, not professional advice. Two distinct
disclaimers are displayed:
13.1 Legal disclaimer
"This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. For advice
specific to your situation, please consult a qualified attorney or contact Legal Aid South Africa (0800
110 110)."
13.2 Financial disclaimer
"This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute financial advice.
MzansiLegal is not a registered financial services provider under the FAIS Act. For personalised
financial advice, consult a registered financial advisor. For debt counselling, contact the National
Credit Regulator (0860 627 627)."
13.3 Contract analysis disclaimer
"Contract analysis is provided for informational purposes only. AI-generated insights may not capture
all legal nuances. Always have important contracts reviewed by a qualified attorney before signing."
All disclaimers are pre-translated into the 4 supported languages and displayed with every
AI-generated response.
14. Success metrics
• AI answers include accurate, verifiable citations in ≥90% of responses
• Cross-domain questions answered holistically with citations from both domains
• Multilingual support with ≥95% language detection accuracy
• Voice input transcription accuracy ≥90% (English, Afrikaans), ≥80% (isiZulu, Sesotho)
• Voice output produces natural-sounding speech in all 4 languages
• Contract analysis correctly identifies contract type in ≥90% of uploads
• Red flag detection cites accurate legislation references
• Platform fully operable via keyboard and screen reader (WCAG 2.1 AA target)
• Dyslexia-friendly mode available with one-click toggle
• Average latency under 8 seconds (text), under 12 seconds (voice)
• Knowledge base covers 13+ documents (7 legal + 6 financial)
• Admin can upload and index a new document within 2 minutes
• Full CI/CD pipeline with automated deployment via Azure DevOps
