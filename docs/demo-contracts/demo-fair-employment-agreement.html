EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

Entered into between:

Globex Logistics (Pty) Ltd (Hereinafter referred to as the "Employer")

and

Jane Doe (Hereinafter referred to as the "Employee")

1. Commencment and Position

1.1 The Employee is appointed as a Warehouse Picker starting 01 May 2026.

1.2 The first 3 months of employment are considered a probationary period. Termination during or at the end of probation will be carried out in accordance with Schedule 8 of the Labour Relations Act, requiring a fair procedure and minimum statutory notice.

2. Remuneration

2.1 The Employee will be paid at a rate of R27.58 per hour (in compliance with the National Minimum Wage Act), payable monthly in arrears on the last working day of the month.

2.2 No deductions may be made from the Employee's remuneration without the Employee's written consent, except for statutory deductions (e.g., UIF, PAYE). Any deduction for damage or loss may only occur after a fair procedure where the Employee is given a reasonable opportunity to show why the deduction should not be made, and deductions may not exceed 25% of the Employee's remuneration.

3. Working Hours and Overtime

3.1 The standard working hours are 45 hours per week (Monday to Friday, 08:00 to 17:00).

3.2 Overtime is voluntary and may only be worked by prior agreement. Overtime will be remunerated at 1.5 times the normal hourly wage.

3.3 The Employee is entitled to a continuous meal interval of 60 minutes after 5 hours of continuous work.

4. Annual Leave and Sick Leave

4.1 The Employee is entitled to 15 working days of paid annual leave per annual leave cycle (or 1 day for every 17 days worked).

4.2 The Employee is entitled to 36 days of paid sick leave in every 36-month cycle.

4.3 A medical certificate is only required if the Employee is absent for more than two consecutive days, or on more than two occasions during an 8-week period.

5. Family Responsibility Leave

5.1 After 4 months of employment, the Employee is entitled to 3 days of paid family responsibility leave per annual cycle (for the birth/sickness of a child, or death of an immediate family member).

6. Uniform

6.1 The Employer will provide the necessary safety uniform at no cost to the Employee.

7. Termination

7.1 Notice of termination must be given in writing as follows:

  • 1 week, if employed for 6 months or less
  • 2 weeks, if employed for between 6 months and 1 year
  • 4 weeks, if employed for 1 year or more.

7.2 All terminations for misconduct, incapacity, or operational requirements will be subject to substantively and procedurally fair processes as outlined in the Labour Relations Act.


Signed at Johannesburg on 25 April 2026
